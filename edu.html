Hybrid Education Overview

We provide flexible digital and in-school learning support to children and youth across Nairobi, Murang‚Äôa, Kiambu, Nakuru, Machakos, and Mombasa.

Current Status: Our Hybrid Education Centres are already established and operational, offering structured digital learning, local mentorship, and access to international academic content.

The centres combine physical classrooms with digital platforms, AI-supported learning, and live virtual sessions from global educators. This model ensures continuity, quality, and affordability for learners who would otherwise be excluded from tertiary education.

Support Needed: To fully equip and scale these centres, we seek donor support to strengthen classroom infrastructure, expand internet capacity, and subsidize learning costs for students who cannot fully afford fees.

Why Hybrid Learning?

Sub-Saharan Africa has a tertiary education enrollment rate of under 10%, compared to over 70% in developed regions. Traditional institutions often fail due to cost, distance, and infrastructure challenges.

  • World-class digital content from international universities
  • Live online sessions with professors and entrepreneurs
  • On-site mentoring and guided study sessions
  • AI-supported personalized learning and assessment
  • Lower cost per student through shared infrastructure

This hybrid approach makes quality education accessible while remaining financially sustainable with donor and partner support.
